# World of Oakhaven - Lore Summary

## World Information

**World Name**: Oakhaven

**Setting**: Low-magic, post-collapse forest kingdom. After a catastrophic magical event, magic became rare and dangerous. The kingdom operates in the ruins of a once-greater realm.

**Technology Level**: Medieval. No gunpowder, no steam engines, no advanced metallurgy. Weapons are steel (from pre-collapse), leather armor, and wooden weapons. Communication is messengers or mounted riders.

**Magic System**: 
- **Frequency**: Rare - only a few individuals per generation show magical ability
- **Cost**: Taxing on the body - magical users suffer physical toll (bleeding eyes, hair loss, skin burns)
- **Society View**: Feared by commoners, hunted by The Iron Circle
- **Forms**: Elemental manipulation, healing, necromancy, illusion

## Key Factions

### The Iron Circle
- **Role**: Military enforcement, hunter of magical practitioners
- **Technology**: Steel armor, swords, crossbows, basic siege weapons
- **Beliefs**: Magic is a corrupting force that must be contained
- **Symbols**: Iron sigil, hammer and anvil

### The Root-Walkers
- **Role**: Druidic cult, guardians of ancient forests
- **Technology**: Stone tools, leather armor, simple farming implements
- **Beliefs**: Nature and ancient spirits guide all living things
- **Symbols**: Tree ring patterns, woven branches

### The Freefolk
- **Role**: Common villagers who avoid magic and faction politics
- **Technology**: Basic medieval tools, pottery wheel, simple forges
- **Beliefs**: Survival above all, distrust of both factions
- **Symbols**: None, wear simple cloth

### The Arcane Conclave
- **Role**: Scholarly faction studying magical history
- **Technology**: Ancient texts, alchemical apparatus, magical circles
- **Beliefs**: Knowledge is power, study magic for understanding
- **Symbols**: Open book with arcane symbols

## Magic Rules

**Forbidden**:
- Mass magic (affecting entire populations)
- Magic in public spaces (illegal)
- Necromancy (undead creation)
- Time manipulation
- Reality warping

**Allowed** (with cost):
- Minor healing (personal wounds only)
- Elemental control (small amounts)
- Illusion (deceptive illusions)
- Nature communication (plants only)

## Technology Constraints

**Allowed**:
- Iron and steel weapons (pre-collapse)
- Simple farming tools
- Basic stone masonry
- Candlelight and fire pits

**Forbidden**:
- Gunpowder weapons
- Steam engines
- Advanced metallurgy
- Magic-powered machinery

## Character Creation Guidelines

### Valid Faction Examples
- "A freeborn villager from Oakhaven"
- "A former Iron Circle soldier"
- "A Root-Walker druid"
- "An exile from the Arcane Conclave"

### Invalid Faction Examples (will trigger lore conflict)
- "A magical elf of ancient blood"
- "A steampunk engineer"
- "A cybernetically enhanced warrior"
- "A member of The Shadow Legion"

### Valid Motivation Examples
- "Survive the collapse"
- "Protect the forests"
- "Find the source of corruption"
- "Hunt the dark mages"

### Invalid Motivation Examples
- "Conquer with technology"
- "Destroy all magic" (too broad)
- "Rebuild the machine age"

### Valid Goal Examples
- "Find a safe place for my people"
- "Discover why magic faded"
- "Save the dying forest grove"
- "Learn the old ways from the spirits"

### Invalid Goal Examples
- "Build a steam empire"
- "Create an undead army"
- "Time travel to the golden age"

## World History (Brief)

**The Great Collapse**: 50 years ago, magic overloaded and nearly destroyed the world. Survivors learned to live in a low-magic era.

**Current Era**: The Age of Ash. Magic exists but is dangerous. People fear it but need its rare benefits.

**Faction Wars**: The Iron Circle hunts magic users. The Root-Walkers protect the forests from industrial encroachment.

## Tone and Style

**Atmosphere**: Dark fantasy, melancholic, survival-focused. The world is dying slowly, and characters must choose how to survive.

**Narrative Voice**: Third-person limited. Descriptions should emphasize the cost of magic and the fragility of life.

**Key Themes**: Sacrifice, survival, the cost of power, nature vs. technology, memory of what was lost.

## Example Backstories

### Example 1: Valid
"I am a farmer from the northern groves, though my daughter shows faint magical ability. We live in fear of The Iron Circle hunters, but also need her gift when drought strikes. I seek to find a way to teach her control without attracting the soldiers' attention."

### Example 2: Invalid (Technology)
"I am a brilliant inventor from the old cities, seeking to rebuild humanity with steam and gears. My inventions will restore our prosperity without relying on dangerous magic!"

**Issue**: Steam technology is forbidden in this world.

### Example 3: Invalid (Magic Rules)
"I am a powerful wizard of the Void Legion, master of time and death! With a snap of my fingers I can raise an entire army of the dead!"

**Issues**: 
- Faction doesn't exist
- Necromancy and time magic are forbidden
- "Void Legion" is not a recognized group

### Example 4: Valid (Low Magic)
"I am a young Iron Circle recruit, tasked with hunting down the elusive Root-Walkers. My squad captain once showed me a druid who could heal wounds but bled from her eyes afterward. I must decide: hunt the magic users or join their cause?"
